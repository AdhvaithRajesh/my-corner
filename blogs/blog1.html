I don't know how this is not a rant. I am rather proud. Surprised at myself for not being annoyed at Sri Benaka Cameras - Professional Camera Repair & Service. It's because I didn't expect them to return my camera today. My expectation was not misplaced. I had called them at 10am today. They told me it's Ready. "Shall I come at 2 or 3pm?", I asked. The Woman said Ok and hung up.

I'm at Sri Benaka Cameras - Professional Camera Repair & Service at 2:30pm. The Woman is not. My camera is not. The Woman had apparently looked at an outdated ledger which had marked my camera as Ready. This Ready was the Ready for the last time they had called and told me it was Ready. It wasn't Ready then either. At least they had my camera with them then.

I asked them why didn't they didn't have my camera. They said it's being repaired. They said "miscommunication happened" with no sense of apology. I was very happy with their reason because since it was a perfectly valid reason, it was not a waste of my 2 hours and 100 rupees of metro fare.

I walked around and found a familiar General Items. I said hi to my friend there. Her name is Domple (name changed for privacy). I like my relationship with her. I am not connected to her digitally. I have met her twice and the reason I remember her fondly is because she told me to Have a Lovely Day the first time I left the store.

I had a look around the store. This time they had new Cook-And-Story Books. They had Rambutan Jam. I told her my dad grows Rambutan in Kerala. I also told her it has not yet grown. Then we discussed our dads' addiction to The Premier Technology of The World - AI. She said mine's better off because mine's addicted to Gemini. Her's is addicted to ChatGPT. Which is obviously worse. Her father has Artificial Intelligently diagnosed her with several diseases.

We then also discussed a nice thought. Our grandparents are gullible to being scammed online because they aren't digitally comfortable. Our parents are, so you'd think they'd be safe from The Digital Dangers. I think they think they're safe. ChatGPT reassures them that they are. We wondered what would get the better of us. Then I argued that it's nice to be gullible so. There will be two kids of the next generation meeting at General Items discussing us and our new gulliblizing addictions. I personally am very excited to be an unwitting cause of concern for my children.

I told her a long-winded story about my experience with a MNC. A MNC is a beautiful blurred friend who treats you nicely. Smooth face, Smooth Features. Indistinguishable, but in a warm amicable way. All MNCs are our friends. They do what's best for The World. Not because they have the resources to. Not because it is their responsiblity. But because they want to. I shall call my MNC friend Doilette(name changed for privacy). I also told her about my experience with college placements. She was empathetic. Almost as much as she was when I mentioned I came from Whitefield.

I told her about my experience with Sri Benaka Cameras - Professional Camera Repair & Service. She was offended for me. She insinuated that I didn't care enough. Hostility #1. I jokingly pretended to be offended, when inside I was actually offended. She asked me why I didn't go off on Sri Benaka Cameras - Professional Camera Repair & Service. She told me they aren't going to give my camera back. Hostility #2. She asked me why I haven't done something about it, or asked them more aggressively. I didn't tell her that I don't really know how to be aggressive. I didn't tell her I'm only a keyboard warrior. I also didn't tell her that I don't like shouting at people - it gets nothing done (I think I'm just not good enough at it yet. I'll get some practice).

Most people I've encountered in jobs like this don't get paid enough to care if they get shouted at. I told her that I operate on the hope that people do their best. This is partially true. This is a rather idealistic child-like thought I have in my mind. Maybe it's not true. I hope it's true. I think she thinks I'm an idiot now. More than not, I have seen people not do their best. I now realize how stupid I sounded. People never do their best. But who's to blame? In a System with no one to be held accountable, everyone's mad at the people they are sandwiched between.

Domple told me she's in a rush to eat her ice cream so I should shut up now. She told me I can look around the store as long as I don't talk and disturb her Ice Cream Time.

It is a lovely store. Odd store. They have very interesting books with very interesting stories. They have empty Japanese Toolboxes. They have traditional Manipuri Earthenware which is also induction-stove compatible. They have photographs. They have spiced peanut butter. They have Rambutan Jam from Kerala. They have paper weights. They have a very nice storekeeper Domple.

Violating Ice Cream Time silence, she asked me if I'd like a spoon of her mango ice cream. I asked her if this was for making up for the two counts of hostility. I asked her if she had time for another long story. She said Didn't I Ask You to Shut Up? Then she beat me into the ground. Hostility #3. Once I woke up, she said she has work. I asked her what work she has, because I thought her work was to hang out with people who come by.

She told me her work was to hang out with people. But she had other work as well. She was a Storyteller (she hangs out with people). She was a designer. General Items is a design studio. You'd think a designer's job at a Design Studio is to either Design (which she wasn't doing, she was eating Mango Ice Cream) or hang out with people. She told me she's Bland Head and I told her that makes sense. I asked her What and she said Brand Head and I said oh and she told me our hostilities have now evened out.

The long story I didn't tell her was about my experience with my car's service. In short, they severely disappointed me. I did what I do as a keyboard warrior and posted a review. It became popular. Google told me my review has reached 1000 views. I am a proud Local Guide Level 2. Only after the review did my appointed Car Service Manager call me proactively. He said Sir You put Bad Review. I said Yes. Then he said the Zonal Manager (another Sir, the other bread of the People he is sandwiched between) asked to fire him. I said Oh No That is Unfortunate. I meant it. I had no intention of having him fired with my Bad Review.

The only purpose of Bad Review was for people to know about my experience. A sensible response is one of acknowledgement of the mistake, and possibly of the steps taken to ensure non-occurence. It is unfortunate that there might be a good number of people who are happy to hear that their poorly performing Car Service Manager got fired. Reminds of the time when I had a poor bike service and the Bike Service Manager decided to shout at his Bike Service Managee to appease me. Three Sad Men.

I told the Car Service Manager that I neither had any intention of having him fired nor did I want that to happen. I don't think he believed me because he didn't sound less sad. You'd think a valid reason would make one happy. Maybe my reason was not as valid as Sri Benaka Cameras - Professional Camera Repair & Service. I told him that I can speak to his other Sir and tell him that I don't want this firing to happen and that it won't do anyone any good. He instead asked me to come to the service center and sign a customer satisfaction form. I had the time so I went and signed it and was asked to take the review down. They gave me a complimentary car wash. They did not give me a car wash - they actually gave me a coupon to get my car washed by them. I walked out of the service center - dry, unwashed - but soaked heavy with the Car Service Manger's hope that I take the review down.

I did not take the review down. Why should I take the review down?

What my experience with the Car Service makes me feel is a recurring theme in many of the conversations I have with my friends. It's a conversation about The System. It's an odd place, this System. It's been so carefully architected that everything works and everything is broken. Few get credit for building it. No one is accountable for breaking it. We, The People of India, having sullenly been in it for so long have as a group of people forgotten what else The System could be.

We, The People, have gotten so used to releasing our The System-ly frustration at We, The People. Are you honking at the red light or the person in front of you? One is deaf to your horns and The Other has become deaf to it. If The Other hasn't yet become deaf to it, then The Other is probably honking at the person in front of him.

A frustrated person in The System looks for an easy way out. Shouting at someone seems like a way out. Blaming someone seems like a way out. Conning someone seems like a way out. But how happy are you putting someone else down when you're still in the same place after? The more we stay in The System, the less we trust each other. The less we trust each other, the harder we'll find it to make a change to The System. The harder we'll find it to establish accountability.

The System reminds me of one of the times I had a burnout. Quite a difficult paradoxical place to be. You need to get a push to get out of the rut you're in. And you need this push when you feel like you can't push any further. But the push almost always comes from within. It must come from within.

I wish people were lighter and happier and didn't take things this seriously.

After she told me she's a Storyteller, I asked her if she has her work online. I found out that she, like me, is an idiot. She is self-critical and has not published her work. I told her the next time I meet her she should have some work published and I should have some work published. This is me upholding my end of it. I am sure she will uphold her end of it. I kept talking and she once again beat me into the ground. After my non-self-induced nap, I limped out of the store, and she asked me to Have a Lovely Day.
